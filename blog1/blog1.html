About Dandelion Chocolate Four Barrell Café de Olla

Place 1 Dandelion Chocolate 740 Valencia St, San Francisco, CA 94110

When I went to Dandelion Chocolate on a sunny Saturday afternoon, Valencia Street was bustling and thrumming with spring energy. Inside, the intimate store was crowded with people sitting and enjoying their drinks and browsing through the offerings of different chocolate products. There were three different hot chocolate types: regular, Mission style, and European sipping chocolate. I opted for the Mission, which has a special flavor twist of cinnamon, cayenne, and chili. I waited in line for my hot chocolate for about five minutes, but the store was well equipped for lines by placing chocolate samples along the wall to taste as you waited. The Mission hot chocolate ranged from $5.25-$8, depending on what size you order. I suggest getting a small size because it is so rich that getting a large size might give you a tummy ache. My drink was ready within a couple of minutes and I decided to drink it outside in their cute outdoor seating. It was obvious to me that Dandelion Chocolate cares about their presentation because it serves its drinks in innovative to-go cups that have a small, delicious cookie in a carrier on the side. Bonus points for packaging and brand identity! The hot chocolate was perfectly rich and creamy, without being too thick or sweet. I could feel the spice tingling in my tongue and throat, enough to be satisfying without traumatizing any customers. It’s obvious that they take their chocolate seriously as some of the notes of th cocoa came through even though it was not raw chocolate. I was very pleased with my drink and find it perfect for anyone who’s looking for a gourmet hot chocolate or cocoa with a little extra flair and finesse. Bonus points for cute baristas!
