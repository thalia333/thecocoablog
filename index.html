THE MISSION

The Mission District in San Francisco is a vibrant hub of Latinx culture, known for its rich history, colorful street art, and lively atmosphere. The neighborhood is a delicious blend of traditional and contemporary influences, and the streets are pocketed with cozy cafés, making it the perfect spot to savor a cup of hot chocolate. The area's warm and welcoming spaces were why the Mission District stood out to me when I moved to San Francisco, and why - whether you're enjoying it with a touch of cinnamon or as dark and bitter as cacao beans, it is the perfect place to review local hot chocolate.

ABOUT ME

Hi! My name is Thalia Lambros, and I am a lover of a hot chocolate. In this blog, I will be tasting my way around the Mission district’s hot chocolate offerings in a series of three locations. Whether it’s a velvety, rich classic or a unique gourmet twist, there’s nothing I’d rather do than curl up with a perfectly crafted cup of hot cocoa to warm both the body and soul. Being from Missoula Montana, there are no gourmet cafes to pick up a mug of warm cinnamon cocoa or traditional Oaxacan hot choco, which is why I am so excited to take advantage of all the chocolatey goodness SF has to offer. Beginning with this short series of cafes in the Mission district, I’m excited to share my journey of finding the best cocoa spots this part of the city has to offer—one delicious sip at a time.
